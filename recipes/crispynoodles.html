Vietnamese Crispy Fried Noodles

Next Recipe

Treat your taste buds to a delightful Vietnamese classic, Mi Xao Gion, also known as Crispy Fried Noodles. This dish embodies the perfect harmony of textures and flavors with crispy fried egg noodles, succulent stir-fried vegetables, and your choice of protein, all generously coated in a savory, umami-packed sauce. Whether you're a fan of seafood, chicken, or tofu, this dish is a culinary adventure that's both comforting and unforgettable.



Ingredients
  • 8 ounces of resh egg noodles (or dried egg noodles)
  • Vegetable oil for frying
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 small onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 carrot, julienned
  • 1 red bell pepper, julienned
  • 1 cup broccoli florets (or any other vegetable of your choice)
  • 1/2 cup sliced mushrooms (optional)
  • 1/2 cup baby corn(optional)
  • 1/2 cup chicken, beef, shrimp, or tofu (optional)
  • 3 tablespons oyster sauce
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 1/4 cup chicken or vegetable broth
  • 1 teaspoon cornstarch mixed with 2 teaspoons water (for thickening)
  • Fresh cilantro leaves
  • Lime wedges
  • Crushed red pepper flakes(optional)


Instructions

    Prepare the Crispy Noodles

  1. If you are using fresh egg noodles, separate them into individual portions. If using dried egg noodles, cook them according to the package instructions until they are slightly undercooked (al dente). Drain and set aside.

    2. Fry the Noodles

  2. Heat about 1 inch of vegetable oil in a deep skillet or wok over medium-high heat. Once the oil is hot (around 350°F or 175°C), add the noodles one portion at a time. Fry each portion until they turn golden brown and crispy, which should take about 1-2 minutes per side. Remove the crispy noodles and drain them on paper towels. Set aside.

    3. Prepare the Sauce

  3. In a separate bowl, dissolve the cornstarch in water to make a slurry. Set aside.
  4. In a separate bowl, dissolve the cornstarch in water to make a slurry. Set aside.

    5. Stir-Fry the Vegetables and Protein

  5. Heat 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil in a wok or large skillet over medium-high heat.
  6. Add the minced garlic and sliced onion, and stir-fry for about 1 minute until fragrant and the onion begins to soften.
  7. Add your choice of protein (chicken, beef, shrimp, or tofu) and stir-fry until it's cooked through. If using tofu, make sure it's golden brown on all sides.
  8. Add the julienned carrot, red bell pepper, broccoli, mushrooms, bamboo shoots, and baby corn (if using). Stir-fry for about 2-3 minutes until the vegetables are slightly tender but still crisp.

    9. Combine Everthing

  9. Pour the sauce mixture over the stir-fried vegetables and protein. Stir to combine.
  10. Add the chicken or vegetable broth and stir well.
  11. Pour the cornstarch slurry into the wok and stir until the sauce thickens. This should only take a minute or so.

    12. Serve

  12. Place a generous portion of crispy fried noodles on a serving plate. Spoon the stir-fried vegetables and protein mixture over the noodles.
  13. Garnish your Mi Xao Gion with fresh cilantro leaves, lime wedges, and crushed red pepper flakes for some extra heat (if desired).
  14. Serve immediately while the noodles are still crispy. Enjoy your homemade Crispy Fried Noodles!
